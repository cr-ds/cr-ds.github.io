How relevant is the use and the corresponding number of building cards in the deck?

Number of Buildings

There are different types of cards in Clash Royale. One of these card types is buildings. Buildings are cards that can be placed in a stationary position on the playing field and perform defense or offense actions depending on the card. When building a deck, the player is not limited to the number of buildings in the deck. It is therefore possible to build a deck without buildings, but also with one, two or three buildings. It is therefore interesting to know whether there is an influence of the number of buildings on the win rate and what influence this has.


To answer this question, we have presented an overview of the win rate in relation to the number of buildings used for all decks with at least 10 battles in the following violin plot. We have filtered for at least 10 battles here, as we did not want to include one-off outliers that were tested for fun in the analysis. The violin plot shows that decks with one building have the best median win rate of 60.71%. Decks with three buildings, on the other hand, perform the worst with a median win rate of 35.71%. Decks with no or two buildings have a median win rate of 58.33% and 56.04% respectively. The filter also shows that in a serious number of games there are no decks that use more than three buildings.


Based on these results, it can be said that the number of buildings is definitely relevant for the win rate. However, this relevance is not particularly high.

Specific Buildings

As previously mentioned, there are also more precise distinctions to consider when it comes to buildings. We distinguish between siege towers, defensive buildings and spawner buildings. A siege tower is a building that actively attacks the enemy's towers. A spawner building is a building that places troop units on the playing field at certain time intervals and a defensive building is a building that is used to defend your own towers.


In the following Sunburst graphic, we show the individual buildings depending on their building type. The size of the building segment in the graphic depends on the battles played with it. We use a color scale to show the win rate with this building. Both the number of battles and the win rate can also be displayed by hovering over the building. By selecting a building type, the view can be changed so that only the buildings of this type are displayed. Furthermore, we offer the option of filtering the view to the evaluations of decks with one, two or three buildings.


For example, if we look at the graph for decks with one building, i.e. the number of buildings with the highest win rate, we can see that the Tesla is played the most and has a win rate of 56.17%. The Bomb Tower, on the other hand, has almost 20,000 fewer battles in the evaluation, but has a win rate of 60.04%. So it's not just the number of buildings in decks that matters, but also the specific buildings themselves.
